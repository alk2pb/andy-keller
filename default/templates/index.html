About

Academic

My name is Andy Keller and I currently attend the School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS) at the University of Virginia. To tell you a little about my academic career, my primary major is systems engineering with a focus in financial engineering, and I am also working on a minor in engineering business. I have had the honor of being accepted into two scholar programs here at the University. One is the Technology Leadership Program, which is a highly competitive program dedicated to interdisciplinary learning and leadership. The other is the Rodman Scholar Program, which consists of the top 5% of students enrolled in SEAS and is dedicated to outstanding academic performance, sustainability, and community culture and leadership.

Experiential

Much of what I learned during my academic career has been useful during my experiential activities. These activities include being a section leader in the Cavalier Marching Band, being the founder and president of the Math Modeling Group at UVA (which competes every year in the Mathematical Contest in Modeling), being an undergraduate research assistant, and several personal projects.

Passion

My personal projects (now consolidated under the name, Discvry) are my true passion. These projects consist of several ideas that I believe will impact the world for the better. One in particular I feel will be my life’s work. I have a vision of developing a web portal through which an artist can attain any and all of his or her marketing needs, allowing for him or her to focus primarily on creating the art that makes this world so wonderful. My other ideas include a low net worth private wealth management software platform, location based event updates, and crowdsourced education funding, among others. Ever since I can remember, I have wanted to help people (along with perhaps making a name for myself). These projects are my best bet at making peoples’ lives easier and changing the world, and I am utterly dedicated to making them a reality.

Her and I get along very well and we share many of the same interests. For example, as well as our parents, thoroughly enjoy tennis. Still, it is my life goal to start my own technology company that develops products that make the world a better place.

Projects

Capstone: Google Glass in Surgery

I am working with Professor Stephanie Guerlain and Doctor James Forrest Calland on developing applications for Google Glass in surgery. Our primary goal is to discover use cases for Glass that can be utilized in the operating room in order to assist the physician and/or medical student in performing and/or learning/teaching how to perform a surgery. We will also place emphasis on developing applications that assist in the standardization of surgical procedures in order to improve safety in the operating room.

Thesis: What is Google up to?

To a ccompany my capstone project, I am also researching Google's business model and attempting to determine what Google's plans are for the forseeable future. In a recent article, the Wall Street Journal appears to claim that Google is in direct competition with Apple with its new Nexus 6 smartphone. I, on the otherhand, do not believe that Google is competing directly with Apple at all. Furthmore, I claim that Google identifies itself purely as a software company that sells advertisements and that any foray into hardware, operating systems, or another industry is merely an attempt to increase advertisement viewership.

Predictive Technology Laboratory

I assisted Dr. Matthew Gerber of the Predictive Technology Laboratory (PTL) in developing two open source software projects: an open research platform and a crime prediction platform. The open research platform allows researchers and data scientists to access PTL datasets and compete with each other to develop the best forecaster. The crime prediction platform (Asymmmetric Threat Tracker) will allow municipalities to input marginal data in order to predict where crimes are most likely to occur.
